# Public Summary — Ethical AI Strategy for Survivor-Created Records

**Team:** Usyd Applied Engineering — March 2026 — Team 7  
**Client:** Sarah Ailish McLoughlin / EduLinked  

---

## Summary
Team 7 developed a draft Ethical AI Strategy focused on the safe and responsible use of artificial intelligence with survivor-created records.

The project provides practical guidance for organisations to adopt AI while protecting authorship, privacy, and consent. It combines governance recommendations, accessibility design, and a staged implementation approach.

---

## What the team delivered
- Ethical AI strategy tailored to sensitive, survivor-led material  
- Authorship and provenance framework  
- Risk and ethics model (privacy, misattribution, harm prevention)  
- Staged implementation roadmap (pilot → evaluate → scale)  
- Accessible communication outputs (Easy-Read, Auslan script)

---

## Key principles
- Survivor leadership and consent  
- Privacy-first system design  
- Human oversight in decision-making  
- Transparent and traceable processes  
- Accessibility by default  

---

## Why this matters
This work supports organisations working with sensitive records to:
- introduce AI safely  
- improve accessibility  
- maintain trust and evidentiary integrity  
- reduce harm
