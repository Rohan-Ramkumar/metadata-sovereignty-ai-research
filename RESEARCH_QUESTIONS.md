# Research Questions
Metadata Sovereignty Alliance – AI Strategy Project

This document translates the project brief into clear research questions.

Students should use these questions to guide research and divide responsibilities across the team.

Each section corresponds to a research area in the repository.

---

# Research Area 1 — AI Trends in Ethical and Community Technology

Goal: Understand current AI developments relevant to ethical data systems and community organisations.

Research Questions

1. What are the most significant AI trends currently affecting the education, advocacy, and community technology sectors?

2. How are organisations using AI to assist with documentation, knowledge management, or communication?

3. What emerging AI approaches emphasise transparency and explainability?

4. What are the risks associated with applying AI in sensitive data environments?

5. What examples exist of organisations using AI in ethically governed ways?

Output

Students should produce:

• a short summary of relevant AI trends  
• examples of responsible AI use  
• analysis of relevance to this project  

Repository location

/02_background_research/ai-trends.md

# Research Area 2 — Metadata Standards and Authorship Tracking

Goal: Identify data standards that support authorship protection, provenance tracking, and record integrity.

Research Questions

1. What metadata standards exist for documenting authorship and provenance?

2. How do these standards record information such as:
   - author
   - creation date
   - edit history
   - record ownership
   - document purpose?

3. Which standards are most widely used in digital archives or research repositories?

4. How well do these standards support long-term traceability of records?

5. What combination of metadata fields would form a strong “minimum information checklist” for new records?

Output

Students should produce:

• a comparison table of metadata standards  
• a recommended minimum metadata checklist  
• suggested record lifecycle workflows  

Repository location

/03_metadata-standards/

# Research Area 3 — Accessibility-Focused AI

Goal: Identify AI tools that improve accessibility and communication.

Research Questions

1. What AI technologies exist that help convert information into accessible formats?

2. How do systems generate:
   - plain language summaries
   - Easy-Read documents
   - captions
   - alternative text?

3. What data inputs do these AI tools require?

4. What transparency measures are needed so users can understand how AI outputs were generated?

5. What safeguards should exist when AI processes sensitive or personal information?

Output

Students should produce:

• a list of accessibility AI tools or approaches  
• explanation of functional requirements  
• description of required metadata for safe operation  

Repository location

/04_accessibility-ai-features/

---

# Research Area 4 — Technology for Low Connectivity Environments

Goal: Identify technology approaches suitable for limited or unreliable internet access.

Research Questions

1. What are the differences between offline-first systems and low-bandwidth cloud systems?

2. What advantages and limitations does each approach have?

3. How do these systems manage data synchronisation?

4. Which approach is most suitable for community organisations with limited infrastructure?

5. What security considerations arise when storing records locally versus in the cloud?

Output

Students should produce:

• a comparison table of offline vs low-connectivity systems  
• recommendations for suitable architectures  

Repository location

/04_accessibility-ai-features/offline-vs-low-connectivity.md

# Research Area 5 — Ethics and Governance Frameworks

Goal: Develop a checklist to evaluate responsible AI development.

Research Questions

1. What ethical guidelines currently exist for responsible AI?

2. How can systems ensure:
   - consent
   - privacy
   - transparency
   - human oversight?

3. What governance mechanisms support accountability in AI systems?

4. How can organisations audit or review AI outputs?

5. What criteria could be used to approve or reject proposed AI features?

Output

Students should produce:

• an ethical AI checklist  
• evaluation criteria for AI tools  
• governance recommendations  

Repository location

/05_ethics-framework/

# Research Area 6 — AI Strategy Recommendations

Goal: Develop a practical roadmap for responsible AI adoption.

Research Questions

1. What opportunities exist for AI to improve the organisation’s work?

2. What risks should be avoided when introducing AI tools?

3. What capabilities should be prioritised in the short term?

4. What technical infrastructure would be required for future development?

5. How can the organisation avoid dependence on a single AI vendor?

Output

Students should produce:

• strategic recommendations  
• an implementation roadmap  
• risk mitigation strategies
