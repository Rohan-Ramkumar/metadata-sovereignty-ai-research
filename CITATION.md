# Citation Guidelines
Metadata Sovereignty Alliance – Research Repository

This repository contains research conducted as part of a student consulting project exploring ethical AI, accessibility, and metadata-first record systems.

All research contributions should include clear citations for sources used.

---

# Why Citation Matters

Citations help ensure that research in this repository remains:

• transparent  
• verifiable  
• academically credible  
• traceable for future work  

Proper citation also respects the work of original authors.

---

# Citation Style

Students may use a **simple author-date citation style** unless a course instructor requires a specific format.

Example in text:

Smith (2023) explains that metadata standards are essential for maintaining traceable digital records.

or

Metadata standards are widely used to preserve provenance in digital archives (Smith, 2023).

---

# Reference List Format

At the end of research documents, include a **Sources** section.

Example:

## Sources

Smith, J. (2023). *Digital Provenance and Metadata Standards*. Journal of Information Systems.

World Wide Web Consortium (W3C). (2022). *PROV-O: The Provenance Ontology*. https://www.w3.org/TR/prov-o/

National Institute of Standards and Technology. (2023). *AI Risk Management Framework*. https://www.nist.gov/ai-risk-management-framework

---

# Source Types

Preferred sources include:

• academic journals  
• government reports  
• standards organisations (W3C, ISO, NIST)  
• reputable technology documentation  
• accessibility guidelines.

Avoid relying solely on:

• blog posts  
• marketing material  
• unverified web content.

---

# Citing AI Tools

If AI tools are used during research, students should note this transparently.

Example statement:

"This document was prepared with assistance from an AI language model for drafting and editing. All sources were reviewed and verified by the author."

---

# Linking Sources in the Repository

When possible, include direct links to sources so other contributors can easily verify them.

Example:

World Wide Web Consortium – PROV-O Standard  
https://www.w3.org/TR/prov-o/
