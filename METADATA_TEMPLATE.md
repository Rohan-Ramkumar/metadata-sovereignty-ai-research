# Metadata Template
Metadata Sovereignty Alliance – Record Documentation Template

This template provides a standard structure for recording authorship, provenance, and governance information when creating or analysing records.

The aim is to ensure records remain:

• traceable  
• interpretable  
• ethically governed  
• accessible to both humans and AI systems.

Students should use this template when documenting research artefacts, example records, or case studies.

---

# Record Metadata

## Record Title
Name or short description of the record.

Example  
Community Advocacy Meeting Notes – Housing Support Case

---

## Author

Name of the person who created the original record.

Example  
Jane Smith

---

## Author Role

Describe the author’s role.

Examples  
Self-advocate  
Researcher  
Community worker  
Legal advocate

---

## Creation Date

Date the record was first created.

Format

YYYY-MM-DD

Example  
2026-03-15

---

## Document Purpose

Explain why the record was created.

Examples

• documenting an advocacy case  
• recording meeting outcomes  
• summarising research findings  
• recording lived-experience testimony

---

## Consent Status

Indicate whether consent exists for use of the record.

Possible values

Consent granted  
Consent limited  
Consent withdrawn  
Consent unknown

Include notes where necessary.

---

## Version Number

Track record versions.

Examples

v1.0 – original record  
v1.1 – minor edits  
v2.0 – major revision

---

## Change History

Record edits made to the document.

| Version | Date | Author | Change Description |
|--------|------|------|--------------------|
| v1.0 | 2026-03-15 | Jane Smith | Original record created |
| v1.1 | 2026-03-18 | Jane Smith | Minor corrections |

---

## Source References

List supporting sources or related documents.

Examples

• meeting recordings  
• research papers  
• policy documents  
• case documentation

---

## Accessibility Formats

Indicate whether accessible formats are available.

Examples

Easy-Read version available  
AAC summary available  
Plain language summary available  
Captioned media available

---

## Data Sensitivity

Describe the sensitivity level of the record.

Examples

Public  
Internal use  
Confidential  
Highly sensitive

---

## AI Processing Permissions

Indicate whether the record can be processed by AI systems.

Possible values

Allowed for summarisation  
Allowed for accessibility formatting  
Not permitted for AI processing

# Why Metadata Matters

Strong metadata ensures records can remain:

• understandable years later  
• auditable and accountable  
• ethically governed  
• interoperable with other systems

This template supports the **metadata-first approach** being explored in this project.
