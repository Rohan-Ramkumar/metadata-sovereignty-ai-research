# Metadata Sovereignty AI Research Repository

This repository supports a student consulting project for the **Metadata Sovereignty Alliance (EduLinked Pty Ltd)** exploring ethical AI, authorship tracking, and accessibility-focused metadata systems.

The project investigates how artificial intelligence can be applied responsibly in systems designed to preserve survivor-authored records and lived-experience documentation.

## Project Purpose

The consulting team will research and recommend ways to:

1. Record authorship, change history, and provenance for survivor-created records.
2. Identify accessibility-focused AI features and their metadata requirements.
3. Develop an ethical AI framework and strategic roadmap.

## Ethical Principles

This project prioritises:

- preservation of authorship
- accessible communication (Easy-Read / AAC)
- transparent AI systems
- strong governance and consent practices

The project avoids:

- biometric profiling
- facial recognition
- automated clinical or legal decisions
- training models on sensitive records without explicit consent
- vendor lock-in architectures

## Repository Structure

The repository is organised into research phases aligned with the project objectives.

01_project_brief – context and assignment details  
02_background_research – sector research and literature review  
03_metadata-standards – authorship and provenance frameworks  
04_accessibility-ai-features – accessibility AI opportunities  
05_ethics-framework – governance and safety criteria  
06_strategy-recommendations – AI strategy recommendations  
final-report – final report drafts and submission materials
