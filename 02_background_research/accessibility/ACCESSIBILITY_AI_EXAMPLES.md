# Accessibility AI Examples

This document provides examples of AI technologies that support accessibility.

Students should examine how these tools work, what information they require, and how they could be used responsibly in systems that preserve authorship, consent, and metadata governance.

---

# Plain Language and Text Simplification

AI can simplify complex text to improve readability and comprehension.

Possible uses include:

• converting complex reports into plain language  
• summarising policy documents  
• helping users understand long records  

### Example Technologies

OpenAI language models  
Large language models capable of generating summaries and simplified text.

IBM Watson Natural Language Processing  
Provides text analysis and summarisation capabilities.

# Easy-Read Generation

Some systems are beginning to use AI to help generate Easy-Read content.

This may include:

• simplified text  
• structured formatting  
• symbol-supported documents  

### Example Approaches

Large language models for simplified writing  
Tools that convert complex documents into simplified language.

Symbol-supported document generators  
Software that integrates symbols into accessible text formats.

# Caption Generation

AI can automatically generate captions for audio or video content.

Captions support accessibility for people who are:

• Deaf or hard of hearing  
• watching content without sound  
• processing information better through text

### Example Technologies

YouTube automatic captions  
Speech recognition systems that generate captions.

OpenAI Whisper  
Speech-to-text AI model capable of generating transcripts.

# Important Ethical Considerations

Accessibility AI systems should always include:

• human review of generated content  
• clear authorship attribution  
• consent for processing sensitive records  
• transparency about AI use  

AI should **support accessibility**, not replace human communication or decision-making.

---

# Research Tasks for Students

Students analysing accessibility AI should consider:

1. What accessibility problem does the AI tool address?

2. What information does the system require?

3. What metadata should accompany records before AI processing?

4. What risks exist when applying AI to sensitive records?

5. What human oversight should be required?
