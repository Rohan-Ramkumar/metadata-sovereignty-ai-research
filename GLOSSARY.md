# Glossary
Metadata Sovereignty Alliance – Key Terms

This glossary defines important terms used throughout the project repository.  
Students should refer to this document when encountering unfamiliar concepts.

---

## Accessibility

Design practices that ensure information and services are usable by people with diverse abilities, including cognitive, sensory, and physical differences.

In this project, accessibility includes formats such as:

• Easy-Read documents  
• plain language summaries  
• captions and transcripts  
• alternative text for images  
• AAC-supported communication.

---

## AI (Artificial Intelligence)

Computer systems capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as language processing, pattern recognition, and summarisation.

In this project, AI is considered **a support tool**, not a replacement for human judgement.

---

## AI Transparency

The ability for users to understand how an AI system generated an output.

Transparency may include:

• source references  
• generation timestamps  
• model explanation notes  
• human review status.

---

## Authorship

Identification of the person or group responsible for creating a record or document.

Preserving authorship helps ensure:

• accountability  
• traceability  
• recognition of lived experience.

---

## Consent

Permission granted by an individual to collect, store, or use information related to them.

Consent must be:

• informed  
• voluntary  
• revocable.

---

## Data Governance

The policies and practices that control how data is managed, accessed, and used within an organisation.

Good governance includes:

• access control  
• audit logging  
• consent management  
• ethical oversight.

---

## Easy-Read

A communication format designed to improve accessibility for people with cognitive or language processing differences.

Easy-Read materials typically include:

• simple language  
• short sentences  
• supporting images or symbols.

---

## Human Review

A process in which a person reviews AI-generated content before it is used or published.

Human review helps ensure:

• accuracy  
• ethical appropriateness  
• respect for author intent.

---

## Metadata

Information that describes a record or document.

Examples of metadata include:

• author name  
• creation date  
• document purpose  
• consent status  
• version number  
• edit history.

Metadata helps ensure records remain understandable and traceable over time.

---

## Metadata Sovereignty

An approach to information management that prioritises the preservation of authorship, consent, and contextual meaning within data systems.

Metadata sovereignty emphasises:

• traceable records  
• ethical governance  
• durable system memory.

---

## Provenance

Information describing the origin and history of a record.

Provenance tracking may include:

• who created the record  
• when it was created  
• how it has changed over time.

---

## Record Lifecycle

The stages a record passes through from creation to archiving.

Typical stages include:

1. Creation  
2. Storage  
3. Revision  
4. Use  
5. Archival or deletion.

---

## RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)

An AI architecture where language models generate responses using external knowledge sources rather than relying solely on training data.

RAG systems help improve:

• factual accuracy  
• traceability of information sources.

---

## Version Control

A method for tracking changes to documents or code over time.

Version control helps maintain:

• revision history  
• accountability for edits  
• recovery of earlier versions.

GitHub is a commonly used version control platform.

---

## Systems Memory

The ability of an information system to preserve context and history over time.

In this project, systems memory is supported through:

• metadata  
• version tracking  
• provenance records.
