# Student Research Guide
Metadata Sovereignty Alliance (EduLinked Pty Ltd)

## Welcome

This repository supports a consulting research project exploring how artificial intelligence can be applied responsibly in systems designed to preserve authorship, accessibility, and ethical data practices.

The organisation you are supporting works with **survivor-authored records, lived-experience documentation, and accessible communication formats**. The goal is not simply to apply AI, but to ensure AI systems **respect authorship, consent, and transparency**.

Your work should help design AI systems that strengthen ethical record-keeping rather than replacing human judgement.

---

# Project Context

The Metadata Sovereignty Alliance focuses on:

• protecting authorship and traceability of records  
• accessible communication formats such as Easy-Read and AAC  
• ethical data governance and consent  
• building systems that preserve “institutional memory”  

The project explores how AI can assist these goals while maintaining strong ethical safeguards.

---

# Core Principle: Metadata First

Most AI projects focus on algorithms.

This project focuses on **metadata infrastructure**.

Metadata means the information that describes a record, such as:

• author  
• creation date  
• purpose  
• consent status  
• edit history  
• version number  

Strong metadata allows records to remain:

• traceable  
• auditable  
• ethically governed  
• interpretable by humans and machines.

---

# What We Are Trying to Build

Your research should help inform a system that could eventually support:

• survivor-authored archives  
• accessible documentation tools  
• ethical AI summarisation systems  
• advocacy and case documentation workflows  

The aim is **supporting human advocacy**, not automating decisions.

---

# Objective 1 — Metadata and Authorship Tracking

Research how systems can record:

• authorship  
• creation dates  
• change history  
• record purpose  
• consent status  

Look at existing standards such as:

• Dublin Core  
• W3C PROV-O (Provenance Ontology)  
• PREMIS digital preservation metadata  
• schema.org metadata  

Your task is to identify which elements would best protect authorship and record integrity.

---

# Objective 2 — Accessibility-Focused AI

Research AI tools that support accessibility, such as:

• plain-language summarisation  
• Easy-Read format generation  
• caption generation  
• alternative text generation  

For each AI feature identify:

• what information it requires  
• how authorship is preserved  
• how outputs remain transparent  
• what human oversight is required.

Accessibility and clarity are more important than technical novelty.

---

# Objective 3 — Ethics and Governance

Develop an ethics framework covering:

• consent
• privacy
• transparency
• human review
• authorship preservation

The system must **avoid harmful AI practices** such as:

• biometric profiling  
• facial recognition  
• predictive risk scoring  
• automated legal or clinical decisions.

Your framework should provide simple pass/fail criteria to evaluate AI features.

---

# Research Expectations

When conducting research:

Use credible sources such as:

• academic publications  
• government and NGO reports  
• accessibility standards  
• reputable technology documentation.

Avoid relying solely on blogs or promotional materials.

All claims should include **clear citations**.

---

# Writing Style

Research outputs should be:

• clear  
• structured  
• concise  
• accessible to non-technical readers.

Remember that this project involves **community organisations and lived-experience communities**, not only technical audiences.

Plain language is encouraged.

---

# Documentation Requirements

Each research document should include:

Author  
Date  
Sources  
Summary  

Major revisions should include a short **change note**.

---

# Questions to Guide Your Thinking

As you conduct research, consider:

• How can authorship remain visible in AI systems?  
• How can records remain interpretable years later?  
• What information must always accompany a record?  
• How can AI support accessibility rather than replacing human communication?

---

# Final Goal

Your research should help the organisation make **responsible, well-informed decisions about AI adoption**.

The final recommendations should prioritise:

• ethical governance  
• accessibility  
• transparency  
• sustainability  
• interoperability with other systems.
## Project Task Management

The student consulting team should create a GitHub Project board to manage research tasks.

Steps:

1. Open the repository.
2. Click the **Projects** tab.
3. Create a new project board.
4. Add the following columns:

- To Research
- In Progress
- Needs Review
- Completed

Create task cards for each research area in the project.

Example tasks:

• AI Trends Research  
• Metadata Standards Review  
• Accessibility AI Tools  
• Case Studies  
• Offline vs Low Connectivity Systems  
• Ethics Framework  
• Strategy Recommendations

Each task should be linked to a GitHub Issue so research progress and sources can be documented.
